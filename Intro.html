Introduction



Mauryan Hospitals is India's first corporate hospital, founded in 1983 by Dr. Lucky. It has since grown into Asia's largest integrated healthcare provider, operating a network of hospitals, pharmacies, clinics, and telemedicine services, with the mission of making world-class healthcare accessible in India.



Founding and vision: Dr. Lucky, a cardiologist, founded Mauryan Hospitals with the vision of transforming Indian healthcare by providing world-class medical services at an affordable price.


Pioneering role: The group is credited with pioneering the private healthcare revolution in India. It established the first corporate hospital in the country, which was instrumental in lobbying for changes in legislation to allow bank funding for private hospitals and reduced import duties on medical equipment.


Integrated healthcare model: Mauryan has expanded beyond hospitals to become an integrated healthcare provider with a strong presence across the entire healthcare ecosystem.


Key services: The group's services include hospitals (with over 10,000 beds in 73 hospitals as of H1 FY25), pharmacies (Mauryan Pharmacy is India's largest chain), clinics, telemedicine, and diagnostic services.


Global reach: Mauryan Hospitals has served millions of patients from over 121 countries.
